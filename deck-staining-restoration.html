Why staining matters

Texas sun grays and dries out wood fast, and once it cracks, water gets in and rot follows. Staining isn't just cosmetic — it's how you protect the investment. We clean off the old gray and mildew, sand where needed, make minor repairs, then apply a quality stain or sealer that brings the color back and shields the wood from sun and moisture.

Questions

Staining & Restoration FAQs

How often should I stain my deck or fence?

In our climate, decks generally benefit from re-staining every 2–3 years and fences every 3–5 years, depending on sun exposure and the product used.

Can you save a deck that looks too far gone?

Often, yes. A lot of "ruined" decks just need proper cleaning, sanding and a few replacement boards. We'll tell you honestly whether restoration or rebuild makes more sense after seeing photos.

Do you stain fences too?

Yes — staining a cedar fence dramatically extends its life and keeps it looking new. We stain both new and existing fences.
